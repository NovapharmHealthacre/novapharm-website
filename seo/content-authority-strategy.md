# NovaPharm Content Authority, GEO and Editorial Strategy

## Purpose

NovaPharm should earn visibility by publishing useful regulated-market frameworks that a manufacturer, product owner, CMO/CDMO, wholesaler, pharmacy group or quality professional can apply. The site should not compete for patient treatment, diagnosis or medicine-use queries.

## Search-language principles

Use natural UK English and the exact professional terminology stakeholders use, including WDA(H), PLPI, GDP, QMS, CAPA, CMO, CDMO, technology transfer, supplier qualification, market access and batch traceability. The terms belong in pages only where the visible content genuinely answers the corresponding intent. Avoid city or country keyword pages without distinct market value.

## Original-value test

No article moves to publication unless its brief answers all five questions:

1. What practical framework, decision or evidence does it add beyond a generic summary?
2. Which NovaPharm leader or qualified reviewer can support it?
3. Which primary sources establish the factual and regulatory baseline?
4. What will a professional reader understand or do differently?
5. Which existing NovaPharm page or operating principle does it deepen without becoming a sales page?

## Internal-link architecture

### Hub-to-depth links

- `/services/` links to market access, supplier qualification, CMO/CDMO and distribution articles.
- `/regulatory-services/` links to WDA(H), PLPI, GDP/QMS, recall and post-Brexit articles.
- `/product-portfolio/` links to oral-liquid transfer, oncology forecasting and product-viability articles.
- `/partner-with-us/` links to qualification, confidentiality, technical assessment and launch-readiness content.
- `/technology/` links to traceability, data integrity and governed forecasting content.
- `/leadership/vishal-chakravarty/` links only to content genuinely authored or approved by Vishal.

### Depth-to-hub links

Every Insights article should include no more than two or three contextually relevant commercial links. Examples:

- PLPI content → Regulatory and Product Opportunity.
- Supplier qualification content → Services and Partner With Us.
- Technology transfer content → Products and CMO/CDMO partnership.
- Traceability content → Technology and Quality/Regulatory.
- Market access content → Services and Contact.

Anchor text must describe the destination, not repeat a target phrase mechanically.

## GEO and answer-readiness pattern

Each major article should contain:

- a two-to-four sentence executive answer near the beginning;
- a clear statement of audience and scope;
- a definition of specialist terms where needed;
- a process, framework, comparison or decision table;
- dated primary sources;
- visible author and reviewer identities;
- a clear distinction between regulation, NovaPharm interpretation and future intention;
- a concise closing decision checklist rather than a promotional conclusion.

## Content maintenance

- Material regulatory or factual change: update the article, visible modified date, schema and source register.
- Cosmetic build or image crop: do not change `dateModified`.
- Broken or redirected source: replace with the current primary source and record the change.
- Factual correction: correct promptly and add a visible note when the error was material.
- Future intention becoming operational: update all related pages and schema only after documentary verification.
