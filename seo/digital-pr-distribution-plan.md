# NovaPharm Digital PR, LinkedIn and Off-Site Authority Plan

## Objective

Build corroborating authority through useful expert contribution and consistent official profiles. The plan expressly excludes purchased Knowledge Panels, paid Wikipedia pages, mass press-release syndication, fake awards, link packages, reciprocal schemes and low-quality guest-post networks.

## Distribution sequence for every major Insight

1. Publish the canonical, fully sourced article on NovaPharm.
2. Verify title, canonical, author, structured data, social image and sitemap inclusion.
3. Submit the materially changed URL through IndexNow and request Google recrawl only through Search Console when appropriate.
4. Publish one personal executive LinkedIn post that states the practical problem and one original conclusion.
5. Publish one company-page post focused on the framework or evidence rather than promotion.
6. Publish one visual summary or carousel concept only where it genuinely clarifies a process.
7. Revisit the article after four to six weeks using search/query feedback; do not manufacture freshness.

## Voice

- informed and commercially aware;
- founder-led but not self-congratulatory;
- specific rather than motivational;
- respectful of regulated boundaries;
- clear about current capability versus future intention;
- willing to explain trade-offs, failed assumptions and operational constraints.

## LinkedIn content pattern

### Executive post

- Opening: one concrete observation from the article.
- Middle: the decision framework in three to five sentences.
- Evidence: link to the primary source or full NovaPharm article.
- Close: one serious discussion question.
- Link: canonical URL using the approved UTM convention.

### Company-page post

- Lead with the stakeholder problem.
- Summarise the framework in three points.
- State the relevant regulatory/status caveat.
- Link to the canonical article.

### UTM convention

`utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=organic-social&utm_campaign=<pillar-year-quarter>&utm_content=<article-slug>-<executive|company|carousel>`

Examples:

- `utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=organic-social&utm_campaign=market-access-2026-q3&utm_content=approval-vs-access-executive`
- `utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=organic-social&utm_campaign=plpi-2026-q3&utm_content=plpi-viability-company`

## Backlink and mention audit model

Every discovered reference should be recorded with:

- referring URL and domain;
- date found;
- NovaPharm/Vishal name used;
- linked or unlinked mention;
- destination URL;
- classification: official, independent authoritative, partner, social, directory, low quality, incorrect, duplicate or potentially harmful;
- correction or reclamation opportunity;
- outreach owner and status.

### Priority actions

1. Correct wrong company spelling, logo, URL, CEO title or regulatory status on sources under NovaPharm control.
2. Reclaim legitimate unlinked mentions from reputable publications where a link improves reader verification.
3. Consolidate duplicate directory listings rather than creating more.
4. Decline irrelevant directory, award or backlink offers.
5. Do not disavow links without clear evidence of a harmful pattern and a documented search-quality reason.

## Measurement

Measure quality, not vanity volume:

- authoritative referring domains;
- correctly linked brand mentions;
- qualified organic and referral enquiries;
- article-assisted conversions;
- engagement with original frameworks;
- LinkedIn-to-site sessions by approved UTMs;
- citations or references discovered in search/AI systems, recorded as observations rather than guaranteed outcomes.

Do not report impressions or follower growth as proof of authority without corresponding relevance and qualified engagement.
