# Modern Slavery Act section 54 applicability

Assessment date: 11 July 2026  
Conclusion: mandatory section 54 duty not established on verified information; voluntary policy published

The current official test requires all of the following: a body corporate or partnership; carrying on a business or part of a business in the UK; supplying goods or services; and annual turnover of at least GBP 36 million, including the relevant group calculation.

NovaPharm is a UK body corporate developing the supply of pharmaceutical goods and services. No verified financial evidence available to this implementation establishes consolidated annual turnover at or above GBP 36 million, and the public site describes the company as pre-operational for regulated wholesale supply. The statutory publication duty therefore cannot be asserted.

The Board must reassess annually and after an acquisition, group restructuring or material turnover change. If in scope, the company must publish a financial-year-specific statement describing actual steps, obtain Board approval and director signature, publish dates, and retain prior statements as appropriate.

Source: [UK Government transparency in supply chains practical guide, updated December 2025](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/transparency-in-supply-chains-a-practical-guide/transparency-in-supply-chains-a-practical-guide-accessible).
