# CRO Blind Comparison

Candidate date: 18 July 2026

Status: internal category comparison completed; no independent user panel is claimed

## Method

Thirteen 1440 x 900 Chromium captures were placed in a deterministic random order and relabelled Candidate A through M. A DOM pass replaced organisation names with `Category organisation`, identity-bearing header areas were masked, and logos were hidden before capture. The private comparison set and identity key remain outside the repository at `/tmp/novapharm-cro-blind-dom/`; competitor screenshots are not committed.

The candidate was compared against the recurring public patterns identified across 12 official CRO websites. The evaluator compared first-viewport clarity, sponsor utility, lifecycle depth, responsibility transparency, evidence discipline, visual authority, accessibility and conversion quality. Global scale, trial volume and proprietary infrastructure were excluded from the win condition because NovaPharm cannot evidence them.

Nine competitor captures returned usable 200 responses for the intended category comparison. Allucent returned a 404, the selected PPD/Thermo Fisher URL redirected to an unrelated promotions page and Charles River returned 403. Those three captures were retained as research limitations but excluded from the visual winner decision. Their official public positioning was assessed separately in the benchmark.

This is an internal expert comparison, not a statistically valid blind study or external customer-research panel.

## Category-leader decision

The candidate should not claim category leadership by scale. It is credible as a differentiated, evidence-led coordination proposition for emerging and international developers that need responsibility clarity and UK pathway continuity. Its strongest category advantage is **transparent evidence architecture**, not operational breadth.

The candidate is ready for owner review because it is more useful and more explicit than a generic CRO landing page while remaining inside NovaPharm's documented evidence boundary. Independent sponsor interviews remain the appropriate next validation step before material commercial expansion.
