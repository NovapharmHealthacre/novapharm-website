# Business-plan to website gap analysis

Reviewed: 22 July 2026
Scope: owner-supplied 59-page March 2026 business plan compared with the source-driven public website and secure-platform architecture.

## Decision summary

The website already communicates the legal entity, pre-authorisation boundary, three-pillar sourcing model, PLPI assessment, quality governance, outsourced logistics model, CRO coordination, leadership and technology maturity. The material public gaps are a specialist oncology continuity narrative and a concrete responsible-AI explanation. These are legitimate additions when framed as governance and readiness, not product promotion or operating achievement.

## Gap matrix

| Theme | Current state | Business-plan signal | Safe website action | Excluded detail |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Oncology | Mentioned in the homepage, portfolio and Insights | Major strategic focus | Create a substantial `/oncology/` category page centred on continuity, evidence and readiness. | Named products, availability, treatment claims, sales targets and customer claims. |
| Development-to-market continuity | CRO page covers evidence-led programme coordination | Plan links formulation, clinical development, regulatory work and supply | Add a bounded continuity model connecting accountable parties and evidence hand-offs. | Trial delivery claims, sponsor status and completed-programme history. |
| Product readiness | Portfolio uses high-level categories | Plan contains formulation, source, batch and launch considerations | Add an interactive but non-promotional readiness matrix. | Approval probability, product scores, confidential dossiers and proposed product names. |
| Controlled temperature | Current site explains GDP principles | Plan emphasises specialist handling | Explain lane qualification, excursion governance, custody and evidence as programme-dependent requirements. | Owned cold chain, active lanes or guaranteed conditions. |
| Oncology partner journey | General partner page exists | Plan envisages manufacturers, product owners, logistics and buyers | Add a qualified oncology engagement pathway and evidence request. | Named partners or contracts. |
| Responsible AI | Technology page distinguishes maturity but lacks a dedicated policy page | Plan describes forecasting and blockchain as differentiators | Add `/technology/ai-governance/` and an evidence-only public search experience. | Operational forecasting, performance improvements, autonomous decisions and blockchain deployment. |
| Public search | Navigation has no integrated evidence search | Plan calls for digital infrastructure | Add instant keyword search and optional on-device semantic retrieval from approved public content. | Portal data, business-plan forecasts, supplier pricing and private files. |
| Internal AI | Portal architecture exists without a model gateway | Plan proposes operational AI | Add a provider-disabled gateway, local Ollama adapter and bounded review prototypes. | Public Ollama access, production automation and unreviewed record writes. |
| Evidence governance | Claims controls exist across reports | Business plan mixes intention, forecast and assertion | Add one canonical public-content evidence register and automated oncology/AI claims tests. | Automatic publication or AI approval. |
| Registered office exposure | Full address appears in structured data | Registered-office fact is public | Retain only where legally and contextually justified; record a solicitor/owner review. | Representing it as a customer-facing, pharmacy, warehouse or operating location. |

## Existing strengths preserved

- Conservative pre-operational and B2B-only wording.
- Explicit boundary between incorporation and pharmaceutical authorisation.
- Current CRO responsibility architecture and sponsor-retained duties.
- Approved leadership naming, governance status and portrait controls.
- Source-driven build, canonical metadata, structured data, sitemap and crawler controls.
- Protected portals, server-side role enforcement, private document controls and synthetic validation data.

## High-risk plan claims withheld

- WDA(H), PLPI or other regulatory approvals.
- Current NHS supply, framework participation, hospital customers or pharmacy network size.
- Product availability, stock, treatment or patient-access outcomes.
- Polar Speed or Marken contractual and operating claims.
- Revenue, margin, customer, transaction, savings or service-level forecasts.
- Operational AI forecasting, blockchain traceability, patents or performance improvements.
- Private product, supplier, pricing, investment, cap-table and immigration information.

## Implementation decision

Proceed with two source-driven public additions: a flagship oncology page and a responsible-AI governance page with evidence-only search. Preserve the business plan as a private strategy source and use independent official sources for regulatory and oncology context.
